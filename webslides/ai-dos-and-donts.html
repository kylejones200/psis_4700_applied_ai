AI Dos and Don'ts

You use AI with intention. You think before you accept answers.

Treat AI as a Collaborator

You ask a question. AI gives an answer. You study the answer.

You do not accept the first reply as final. You ask follow-up questions.

Engage in Dialogue

You refine the prompt. You adjust scope. You compare alternatives.

You shape the output. You stay in control of the process.

Maintain Skepticism

You read with care. You verify facts. You check numbers.

You review logic. You look for bias. You take responsibility.

Use Iteration as a Method

You begin with a draft. You improve the draft step by step.

You request clarity. You request depth when needed.

Frame Clear Questions

You state the goal. You define constraints. You provide context.

You avoid vague prompts. You write with precision.

Evaluate Quality with Purpose

You ask whether the answer fits the task. You test reasoning.

You judge correctness. You judge usefulness. You judge truth.

Do Not Dismiss After One Bad Answer

You receive a weak response. You do not give up.

You refine the prompt. You guide the model toward better work.

Protect Integrity

You add your insight. You explain choices in your voice.

You cite when needed. You keep accountability.

Match Tool to Task

You use AI where it fits. You keep judgment with people.

You respect limits. You apply AI where it adds value.

Think Before Automation

You define success. You confirm the objective.

You let AI help with execution. You own direction.

Learn Through Reflection

You review the session. You note what worked. You note what failed.

You improve practice over time.

End

You treat AI use as a disciplined craft.
